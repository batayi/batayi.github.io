Remote Sensing
Study areas: Laos & Colombia · Sensors: Sentinel-1 · Tools: Python, PyTorch, Google Earth Engine

Overview

This project explores how to predict near-term deforestation using radar imagery and environmental variables. I combined Sentinel-1 time series with auxiliary layers (topography, accessibility, previous alerts) to train a deep learning model that highlights areas at risk of forest loss.

Data

  • Sentinel-1 VH/VV time series (backscatter, coherence).
  • Environmental covariates: elevation, slope, distance to roads/settlements, protected areas.
  • Historical deforestation alerts used as labels/targets.

Method

  • Data preprocessing and feature engineering in Google Earth Engine.
  • Patch-based deep learning model implemented in PyTorch.
  • Evaluation using ROC/AUC and spatial validation in different regions.

Results

The model captured spatial patterns of deforestation risk around roads, edges of protected areas, and frontier expansion zones. The results can support prioritising monitoring and enforcement in vulnerable regions.
