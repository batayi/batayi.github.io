Global Data
Scope: Global 1980s–2010s · Data: Forest cover, GMO adoption, population, GDP
Tools: R, Python, GDAL, QGIS, multi-band GeoTIFFs

Overview

This project builds a global multi-year panel linking forest cover, GMO crop adoption, and socio-economic variables at national and subnational levels. The goal is to provide a consistent dataset for analysing long-term interactions between agriculture and forests.

Data Processing

  • Yearly forest cover rasters harmonised and stacked into multi-band GeoTIFFs.
  • GMO adoption maps by crop and year, normalised against crop area or yields.
  • Administrative boundaries and ecological zones (tropical vs non-tropical) used for aggregation.
  • Panel construction in R: ADM1/national, tropical/non-tropical splits.

Technical Stack

  • R for yearly aggregation and panel creation (`.RData` outputs).
  • Python for raster normalisation, masking, and multi-band stack generation.
  • GIS tools (QGIS, GDAL) for visual QC and map outputs.

Applications

The dataset enables analyses of how GMO adoption and agricultural intensification relate to deforestation patterns, especially in tropical regions. It is structured for regression modelling and exploratory mapping.
