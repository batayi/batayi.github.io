UAV Mapping
Client: Zipline · Location: Multiple regions across Ghana
Role: Project lead for UAV data acquisition and orthomosaic creation
Tools: DJI Phantom 4 RTK · Photogrammetry software (e.g. Agisoft Metashape) · GIS viewers

Overview

  • Goal – Map the surroundings of health facilities to give Zipline a clear understanding of terrain and obstacles before sending health-delivery drones.
  • Data – High-overlap UAV imagery over ~250 × 250 m areas centred on each facility, covering buildings, open spaces and nearby obstacles.
  • Workflow – Site selection with Zipline → flight planning → UAV image capture with DJI Phantom 4 RTK → photogrammetric processing → orthomosaics ready for route planning.
  • Result – High-resolution orthomosaics that Zipline planners can use to design safe, efficient flight paths for emergency medical deliveries.

Data Acquisition

  • Worked with Zipline to identify priority health facilities across several regions in Ghana.
  • Planned UAV missions to cover approximately 250 × 250 metres around each facility, capturing both the building and surrounding terrain.
  • Flew a DJI Phantom 4 RTK with high forward and side overlap to ensure accurate photogrammetric reconstruction.
  • Ensured consistent altitude and coverage so that planners could clearly see potential landing approaches and obstacles.

Processing & Mapping

  • Processed imagery in photogrammetry software (e.g. Agisoft Metashape) to generate high-resolution orthomosaics.
  • Georeferenced outputs so they could be easily integrated into Zipline’s internal planning tools and GIS viewers.
  • Checked image quality, alignment and coverage around each health facility to ensure reliable inputs for flight planning.

Impact & Results

  • Provided Zipline with clear, up-to-date maps of the terrain around health facilities, including open areas, trees and built-up zones.
  • Helped planners understand which approach directions were safest before dispatching their health-delivery drones.
  • Improved the reliability and safety of emergency medical drops by giving operations teams a spatial view of each site.
